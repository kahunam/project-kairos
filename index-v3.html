1. Set Up a Logical Site Structure

Why it's important for SEO: A logical site structure helps search engines understand and index your content more effectively. It also improves user experience, which can lead to better engagement metrics and potentially higher rankings.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in navigation and collection features to organise your products into categories and subcategories. Ensure your main navigation menu reflects your site structure.

Working with a developer: A developer can help create custom navigation solutions, implement breadcrumbs, and ensure proper internal linking throughout your site.

Advantages of using a developer: They can create more complex hierarchies and custom solutions that may not be possible with Shopify's default tools, potentially improving both SEO and user experience.

2. Customise Your Store's URL

Why it's important for SEO: A custom URL that includes your brand name is more memorable and professional, which can indirectly benefit SEO by increasing direct traffic and brand searches.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in domain settings to change your URL. This is a straightforward process that most users can handle without developer assistance.

Working with a developer: Generally not necessary for this task, as Shopify provides user-friendly tools for URL customization.

Advantages of using a developer: Not applicable for this task; Shopify's built-in tools are sufficient.

3. Improve Your Shopify Store's Speed

Why it's important for SEO: Page speed is a ranking factor for search engines and significantly impacts user experience, which can affect engagement metrics and conversions.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in image compression, minimise apps, and choose a fast, responsive theme. Run speed tests using tools like Google PageSpeed Insights.

Working with a developer: A developer can optimise code, implement advanced caching techniques, and fine-tune server responses for better performance.

Advantages of using a developer: They can achieve more significant speed improvements through technical optimizations that go beyond Shopify's built-in tools.

4. Find Relevant Keywords

Why it's important for SEO: Targeting the right keywords helps your content appear in relevant searches, driving targeted traffic to your store.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in SEO tools and free keyword research tools to identify relevant terms. Focus on product-specific and long-tail keywords.

Working with a developer: Not typically necessary for keyword research, but a developer could help integrate advanced keyword tracking tools.

Advantages of using a developer: Minimal for this task; keyword research is primarily a strategic rather than technical task.

5. Create Topic Clusters

Why it's important for SEO: Topic clusters help establish topical authority and improve internal linking, which can boost overall site visibility in search results.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's blog feature to create pillar content and supporting articles. Manually interlink related content.

Working with a developer: A developer can create custom content structures and automate internal linking based on topic relationships.

Advantages of using a developer: They can implement more sophisticated content organisation and interlinking systems that go beyond Shopify's default capabilities.

6. Write and Publish Blog Posts

Why it's important for SEO: Regular, high-quality content helps establish authority, targets long-tail keywords, and provides opportunities for internal linking.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in blog feature to create and publish posts. Focus on topics relevant to your products and audience.

Working with a developer: A developer can customise your blog layout, add features like related posts, and implement structured data for blog content.

Advantages of using a developer: They can enhance the blog's functionality and appearance beyond Shopify's default options, potentially improving user engagement and SEO.

7. Optimise Category and Product Pages

Why it's important for SEO: Well-optimised category and product pages are crucial for ranking in product searches and driving targeted traffic.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in SEO fields to add relevant titles, descriptions, and alt text. Ensure product information is comprehensive and unique.

Working with a developer: A developer can implement custom product page layouts, add schema markup, and create advanced filtering options.

Advantages of using a developer: They can create more sophisticated product pages that stand out from competitors and potentially rank better in search results.

8. Fine-tune Your SEO Elements

Why it's important for SEO: Properly optimised SEO elements help search engines understand your content and can improve click-through rates from search results.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in SEO fields to customise titles, meta descriptions, and URLs for each page. Ensure they're compelling and include relevant keywords.

Working with a developer: A developer can implement automated SEO optimization tools and create custom templates for different page types.

Advantages of using a developer: They can set up more advanced SEO systems that save time and ensure consistency across your site.

9. Use Proper Alt Text for Images

Why it's important for SEO: Alt text helps search engines understand images, improves accessibility, and provides opportunities to rank in image searches.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify's built-in alt text fields when uploading images. Describe the image accurately and include relevant keywords where appropriate.

Working with a developer: Generally not necessary for basic alt text implementation, but a developer could create tools for bulk alt text management.

Advantages of using a developer: Minimal for basic implementation, but could be useful for large stores with many images.

10. Implement Structured Data

Why it's important for SEO: Structured data helps search engines understand your content better and can lead to rich results in search pages, improving visibility and click-through rates.

How to get started yourself: Use Shopify apps that add basic structured data. Some themes also include built-in structured data.

Working with a developer: A developer can implement more comprehensive and customised structured data across your site.

Advantages of using a developer: They can create more detailed and accurate structured data implementations, potentially leading to better rich results in search.

11. Build Quality Backlinks

Why it's important for SEO: Backlinks from reputable sites signal to search engines that your content is valuable, which can improve your rankings.

How to get started yourself: Create valuable content that others want to link to. Reach out to industry partners, suppliers, or complementary businesses for potential collaborations.

Working with a developer: Not typically necessary for link building strategy, but a developer could help create linkable assets like tools or interactive content.

Advantages of using a developer: They can create more sophisticated content or tools that are more likely to attract high-quality backlinks.

12. Utilise Shopify Apps

Why it's important for SEO: SEO apps can automate and simplify many optimization tasks, helping you improve your store's SEO more efficiently.

How to get started yourself: Browse the Shopify App Store for highly-rated SEO apps. Start with apps that cover areas where you need the most help.

Working with a developer: A developer can help integrate apps more seamlessly with your store and customise their functionality if needed.

Advantages of using a developer: They can ensure apps work well with your specific store setup and can create custom solutions if existing apps don't meet your needs.

Remember, while many of these tasks can be started on your own using Shopify's built-in tools, working with a developer can often take your SEO efforts to the next level, especially for larger or more complex stores. The key is to balance DIY efforts with professional help where it can make the most impact.
